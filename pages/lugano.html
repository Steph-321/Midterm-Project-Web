About Lugano

Lugano is a city in southern Switzerland‚Äôs Italian-speaking Ticino region. Its Swiss-Mediterranean mix of cultures is closely related to that of Italy‚Äôs northern Lombardy region. This mix is reflected in its architecture and cuisine. The city stands on the northern shore of glacial Lake Lugano, surrounded by mountains. Its main square, Piazza della Riforma, is ringed with pastel-colored, neoclassical palazzi.

Travel Tips

  1. Explore the city: Wander through the old town and visit the Cattedrale di San Lorenzo.
  2. Go on a boat trip: Take a public boat on Lake Lugano for scenic views, or rent a motorboat or paddle boat.
  3. Visit a nearby village: Take a day trip to the charming lakeside village of Gandria or the picturesque town of Morcote.
  4. Via Nassa: This is the main shopping street, featuring arcades and lined with shops.
  5. Souvenirs: Look for Swiss chocolate and watches, or other Swiss products.
  6. Remove your shoes: You must take your shoes off before entering the castle buildings, and plastic bags will be provided to carry them.
  7. Lockers: Lockers are available near the castle if you need to store luggage.
