About St. Moritz

St. Moritz is a high Alpine resort town in the Engadine, Switzerland, known for its luxurious accommodations, outdoor activities, and cultural events. The town is situated at an elevation of about 1,800 meters above sea level and is surrounded by the Albula Alps. It is famous for hosting the Winter Olympics in 1928 and 1948, making it a premier destination for winter sports enthusiasts. St. Moritz offers a diverse range of activities, including skiing, bobsledding, hiking, and more, all set against the backdrop of stunning alpine scenery. The town's rich history, dating back to the Bronze Age, is reflected in its mineral springs and the church of the springs, which were believed to have miraculous healing properties. Today, St. Moritz continues to be a hub for luxury, sports, and nature, attracting visitors from around the world.
