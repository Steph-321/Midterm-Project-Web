About Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbukdong Palace, also known as Gyeongbokgung Palace, is a former royal palace in Seoul, South Korea. Established in 1395, it was the first royal palace of the Joseon dynasty and is now one of the most significant tourist attractions in the country. The palace was among the first landmarks to be established in Seoul and flourished under the 1418‚Äì1450 reign of Sejong the Great. It was destroyed by fire during the Imjin War in 1592 but was restored in 1868. The palace is a symbol of Korea's long and rich history and is known for its Changing of the Guard ceremony, which takes place twice daily.

Travel Tips

  1. Visit on Culture Day: All palaces in Seoul are free on the last Wednesday of each month, plus on some Korean public holidays like Seollal and Chuseok.
  2. Free Admission with Hanbok: Wear a hanbok to enter for free.
  3. Guided Tours: Join free guided tours available in multiple languages.
  4. Changing of the Guards: Attend the Changing of the Guards ceremony, which is held twice a day at 10am and 2pm on Tuesdays.
  5. Royal Palace Pass: Consider purchasing the Royal Palace pass for admission to multiple palaces and Jongmyo shrine.
  6. Best Time to Visit: Visit during the spring or autumn for the best weather and to see the cherry blossoms.
