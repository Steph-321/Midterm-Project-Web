About National Museum

The National Museum of Korea is a testament to Korea's rich history and cultural heritage. It houses a vast collection of artifacts from ancient times to the modern era, including art and culture. The museum's exhibits cover a wide range of topics, from prehistoric times to the present day, and feature both national and international pieces. It also includes a Children's Museum and outdoor grounds with pagodas and stone artworks. The museum is a hub for cultural activities related to collection, preservation, research, and analysis, as well as academic publications and intercultural exchange programs.

Travel Tips

  1. Visitor Guides: The museum offers Visitor Guides in multiple languages to assist visitors. .
  2. Nearby Attractions: The museum is close to Yongsan Family Park and the National Hangeul Museum, making it easy to plan a cultural day in Seoul.
  3. Parking: There is a spacious underground parking lot available for visitors who drive.
  4. Getting There: The museum is easily accessible via subway, with the best route being from Ichon Station (Line 4) Exit 2, which is just a 3-minute walk. For those arriving from Incheon Airport, the Airport Railroad connects to Seoul Station, followed by a transfer to Line 4.
