About Acropolis Museum

The Acropolis Museum in Athens, Greece, is an archaeological museum that houses artifacts from the Acropolis of Athens, spanning from the Greek Bronze Age to Roman and Byzantine times. Designed by architect Bernard Tschumi, the modern structure features extensive glass elements and is built to both display the artifacts and provide a clear visual connection to the ancient Acropolis site itself. Notable exhibits include the Caryatids and the Parthenon Gallery, which displays the Parthenon sculptures, frieze reliefs, and metopes.

Travel Tips

  1. Book in Advance: It's recommended to book your entry tickets in advance to avoid long lines and ensure a hassle-free experience.
  2. Best Time to Visit: The Acropolis is busiest during the peak summer months, so consider visiting during the shoulder seasons of April, May, and October for a more comfortable experience
  3. Crowds and Heat: Expect large crowds, especially in the summer. Arriving early in the morning or late in the afternoon can help reduce the heat and crowds.
  4. Acessibility: The Acropolis Museum is accessible to wheelchair users and people with mobility issues, with ramps and elevators available.
