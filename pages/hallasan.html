About Hallasan mountain

Hallasan is South Korea's tallest mountain, a dormant shield volcano on Jeju Island, while Namsan is a much smaller mountain in Seoul, South Korea, famous for the N Seoul Tower. Hallasan is a national park and a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, standing at about 1,950 meters (6,388 feet), whereas Namsan is about 262 meters (860 feet) high and is a popular city landmark with scenic views and hiking trails.

Travel Tips

  1. Make a reservation: You must book a free reservation online to hike to the summit via the Gwaneumsa or Seongpanak trails.
  2. Check the weather: Consider booking a day or two ahead of time to get a clear view of the weather forecast before your hike.
  3. Pack layers: The weather at the summit can be very different from the base, so pack layers, including a waterproof jacket.
  4. Pack out your trash: There are no convenience stores on the trails, so bring everything you need and be sure to take all your trash with you when you leave.
  5. Stay on the trails: Hallasan has a few different trails; the Seongpanak and Gwaneumsa trails are popular for reaching the summit. Some hikers recommend taking different trails up and down to experience different scenery.
