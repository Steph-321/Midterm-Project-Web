About Bulguksa Temple

Tokyo Tower is an Eiffel Tower-inspired communications and observation tower in Tokyo, Japan, standing 332.9 meters tall and completed in 1958. It is painted white and international orange for air safety and has two observation decks, the Main Deck at 150 meters and the Top Deck at 250 meters. At its base, a four-story complex called FootTown contains shops, restaurants, and museums, while the tower itself also serves as a broadcast antenna for several Japanese media outlets.

Travel Tips

  1. Explore FootTown: Don't miss the shops, restaurants, and attractions located at the base of the tower.
  2. By subway: The closest stations are Onarimon (Mita Line), Kamiyacho (Hibiya Line), and Akabanebashi (Oedo Line), all about a 5-10 minute walk away.
  3. Pack accordingly: Wear comfortable shoes, as you might want to climb the 600 steps to the Main Deck on Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays.
  4. Book tickets: If you plan on taking the Top Deck tour, check your ticket details and confirm the schedule with staff.
  5. Beat the crowds: Visit on a weekday morning or late evening to avoid the largest crowds.
