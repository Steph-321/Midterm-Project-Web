About Himeji Castle

Himeji Castle is a well-preserved Japanese castle, located in Himeji City, Hyogo Prefecture, and is known as the "White Heron Castle" for its elegant white appearance. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a National Treasure, it is celebrated for its masterful feudal-era architecture, elaborate defensive systems, and enduring structure, having survived centuries of history without major damage. The complex, which was completed in 1609, consists of over 80 buildings and features a maze of winding paths to confuse attackers, along with openings for shooting arrows and dropping stones.

Travel Tips

  1. Arrive early: This helps you avoid the longest waits, especially during peak seasons.
  2. Transportation: Himeji Castle is about a 15-20 minute walk from Himeji Station, or you can take a bus or taxi.
  3. Guided Tours: Join free guided tours available in multiple languages.
  4. Kokoen Garden: A combined ticket for the castle and the nearby Kokoen Garden offers a discount.
  5. Remove your shoes: You must take your shoes off before entering the castle buildings, and plastic bags will be provided to carry them.
  6. Lockers: Lockers are available near the castle if you need to store luggage.
