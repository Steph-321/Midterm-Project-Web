About Itsukushima Shrine

Itsukushima Shrine is a historic Shinto shrine on Miyajima Island in Hiroshima, Japan, famous for its "floating" torii gate that appears to float on the water during high tide. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for blending harmoniously with nature, featuring distinctive vermillion buildings constructed over the water on stilts to respect the island's sacred status. The current shrine buildings largely reflect 12th-century construction styles, with the famous torii gate dating to 1875.

Travel Tips

  1. Stay until sunset: The shrine is beautiful when illuminated by lanterns, which you can see from a distance at night.
  2. Allow ample time: A day trip is recommended to fully experience the area and see other attractions on the island.
  3. Go early: Arriving in the morning helps you beat the crowds and enjoy a more peaceful experience.
  4. Check tide times: High tide is best for the classic "floating" view, while low tide lets you walk right up to the torii gate. It's worth seeing at both times.
