About Kyoto Monument

Kyoto's monuments are a collection of 17 UNESCO World Heritage sites that represent the historic imperial capital of Japan and illustrate the development of Japanese culture, particularly in wooden architecture, religious structures, and garden design. These monuments include temples, shrines, and a castle, and are a testament to Kyoto's history as the capital from 794 until the mid-19th century. They showcase a high level of pre-modern Japanese material culture and have significantly influenced Japanese and global traditions.

Travel Tips

  1. Enter respectfully: Don't step on the threshold when entering. Some traditions suggest women enter with their right foot and men with their left.
  2. Participate in rituals: At a shrine, quietly throw a coin into the offering box, ring the bell, bow twice, and clap twice to show respect.
  3. Nature and culture: Explore the Arashiyama region, which includes the famous bamboo forest, a monkey park with great views, and the scenic Sagano Railway or Hozugawa River boat ride.
  4. Use public transport: A one-day subway/bus pass is convenient for getting around the city and can offer discounts at some facilities.
