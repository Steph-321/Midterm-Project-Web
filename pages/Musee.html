About Musee du louvre

The Mus√©e du Louvre is a world-renowned art museum and historical monument in Paris, housed in a former royal palace, that contains a vast collection spanning from ancient civilizations to the mid-19th century. Originally a medieval fortress built by King Philip II, it was later transformed into a royal residence before becoming a public museum after the French Revolution. Today, it is one of the most visited museums globally, famous for iconic works like the Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo, and the Winged Victory of Samothrace.

Travel Tips

  1. Plan Your Visit: Spread your time over the museum's vast galleries to avoid getting lost.
  2. Skip-the-Line Tickets: Purchase online to save time and energy.
  3. Best Times to Visit: Weekday mornings, late afternoons, or Wednesday and Friday evenings are the quietest times.
  4. Off-Season Visits: November to March offers lighter crowds and shorter lines.
  5. Dining Options: Brasseries and bistros offer modern French plates and cozy bistro atmospheres.
